Family Business Succession

Succession Risk Snapshot

Identify where your family business succession could break first.

Most succession failures do not begin with tax, legal, or valuation problems.

They begin earlier when families confuse love, ownership, competence, control, and fairness.

A child can be loved equally without being equally suited to lead.

A shareholder can have economic rights without having operational authority.

A founder can want succession while remaining the central decision bottleneck.

Fairness does not always mean identical treatment.

The question is not only who will succeed.

The question is whether the family, the ownership structure, the governance system, and the business itself are ready for the transfer of power.

A 7-minute diagnostic for family business owners

The Family Business Succession Risk Snapshot helps identify where succession risk is currently concentrated.

It assesses eight critical dimensions:

  • Strategic continuity
  • Leadership succession
  • Ownership and control
  • Governance and decision-making
  • Founder readiness
  • Family alignment
  • Fairness versus equality
  • Emergency continuity

The result is not a generic score. It is a structured view of where the succession process is most exposed.

What the diagnostic helps clarify

The questionnaire is designed to surface questions such as:

  • Is there a credible future leader?
  • Is the business too dependent on the current owner-manager?
  • Are ownership, control, and operational roles clearly separated?
  • Can sensitive topics such as money, competence, inheritance, dividends, or valuation be discussed openly?
  • Could the company operate for 90 days if the current leader suddenly became unavailable?
  • Is keeping the business the best option, or should sale, merger, or professional management be considered?

What you receive

After completing the diagnostic, you may request a short interpretation of your responses.

The Succession Risk Snapshot can include:

  • An overall succession clarity assessment
  • A heatmap of the main risk areas
  • The three most important vulnerabilities
  • Selected red flags
  • A short interpretation of the avoided conversations
  • Recommended next steps
  • A suggested agenda for a confidential advisory discussion

Who this is for

This diagnostic is intended for:

  • Founders and owner-managers
  • Next-generation family members
  • Family shareholders
  • Boards of family-owned companies
  • Families considering succession, professionalisation, sale, or transition

It is particularly relevant when succession has been discussed informally but not yet translated into a clear governance and transition architecture.
